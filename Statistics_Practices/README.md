# Statistics Practices for Behavioral and Experimental Data Analysis

## Overview
This folder contains a structured set of notebooks designed to teach and demonstrate professional use of statistical methods for scientific data analysis. My focus has been on statistical reasoning, inference, hypothesis testing, effect estimation, regression modeling, and reproducible analysis workflows relevant to behavioral and experimental research.

## Folder Structure

- **Practice01_SamplePopulation_Statistics.ipynb** — Population vs sample, parameters vs statistics, sampling variability, and foundations of statistical inference.
- **Practice02_Descriptive_Statistics.ipynb** — Mean, median, mode, variance, standard deviation, IQR, and robustness to outliers.
- **Practice03_Distributions_Statistics.ipynb** — Normal distributions, skewness, kurtosis, sampling distributions, and data visualization.
- **Practice04_CentralLimitTheorem_Statistics.ipynb** — Repeated sampling, sampling distributions of means, and the Central Limit Theorem.
- **Practice05_ConfidenceIntervals_Statistics.ipynb** — Confidence intervals, margin of error, and interval estimation using classical methods.
- **Practice06_HypothesisTesting_Statistics.ipynb** — Null and alternative hypotheses, p-values, Type I and Type II errors, and hypothesis testing workflows.
- **Practice07_EffectSizes_Statistics.ipynb** — Cohen’s d, practical significance, and interpretation of effect size measures.
- **Practice08_tTests_Statistics.ipynb** — Independent and paired samples t-tests, experimental comparisons, and APA-style reporting.
- **Practice09_AssumptionChecking_Statistics.ipynb** — Normality, homogeneity of variance, independence, and diagnostic procedures.
- **Practice10_Correlation_Statistics.ipynb** — Pearson and Spearman correlations, nonlinear relationships, and interpretation of associations.
- **Practice11_Regression_Statistics.ipynb** — Simple and multiple regression, regression diagnostics, and multicollinearity assessment.
- **Practice12_ANOVAPosthoc_Statistics.ipynb** — One-way ANOVA, post-hoc comparisons, Tukey HSD, and group difference analysis.
- **Practice13_BootstrapInference_Statistics.ipynb** — Bootstrap resampling, bootstrap confidence intervals, and comparison with classical inference.
- **Practice14_APAReporting_Statistics.ipynb** — Statistical reporting, effect sizes, confidence intervals, tables, and APA-style results writing.
- **MiniProject01_Replication_Statistics.ipynb** — End-to-end replication project involving data loading, descriptive analysis, visualization, inferential testing, and APA reporting.

## Key Skills Demonstrated
- Statistical reasoning and inferential thinking
- Data exploration and descriptive analysis
- Sampling theory and the Central Limit Theorem
- Confidence intervals and hypothesis testing
- Effect size estimation and interpretation
- Assumption checking and diagnostic procedures
- Correlation and regression modeling
- ANOVA and post-hoc comparisons
- Bootstrap-based statistical inference
- APA-style statistical reporting
- Reproducible and research-oriented data analysis workflows
