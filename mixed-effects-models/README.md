\# Mixed-Effects Models



\## Overview



This folder contains a structured collection of notebooks covering the theoretical foundations and practical application of mixed-effects models for behavioral and cognitive science. Topics progress from the motivation for hierarchical modeling and random effects to model specification, estimation, diagnostics, generalized mixed models, Bayesian multilevel models, simulation-based design analysis, and advanced hierarchical modeling techniques. The emphasis is on conceptual understanding, statistical reasoning, reproducible workflows, and practical implementation using Python for trial-level experimental data.



\## Folder Structure



\* \*\*01-foundations.ipynb\*\* — Foundations of mixed-effects models, hierarchical data structures, repeated measures, clustering, violations of independence, limitations of classical methods, mathematical review, fixed versus random effects, and introductory simulations.

\* \*\*02-random-intercepts.ipynb\*\* — Random intercept models, intraclass correlation (ICC), variance decomposition, shrinkage, BLUP intuition, participant-level predictions, and repeated-measures analysis.

\* \*\*03-random-slopes.ipynb\*\* — Random slope models, subject-specific effects, covariance between random effects, variance-covariance matrices, learning curve simulation, and visualization of individual trajectories.

\* \*\*04-model-specification.ipynb\*\* — Mixed-model formula syntax, nested and crossed designs, interactions, continuous and categorical predictors, contrast coding, centering, scaling, and model specification for common cognitive psychology experiments.

\* \*\*05-estimation-and-inference.ipynb\*\* — Maximum likelihood estimation, REML, optimization, hypothesis testing, likelihood ratio tests, confidence intervals, estimated marginal means, effect sizes, model comparison, and prediction.

\* \*\*06-model-diagnostics.ipynb\*\* — Residual diagnostics, QQ plots, heteroscedasticity, influence diagnostics, leverage, Cook's distance, singular and boundary fits, convergence issues, optimizer selection, identifiability, multicollinearity, and troubleshooting mixed-effects models.

\* \*\*07-longitudinal-and-generalized-models.ipynb\*\* — Longitudinal modeling, growth curves, random growth models, covariance structures, missing observations, generalized linear mixed models, logistic models, Poisson models, negative binomial models, ordinal models, and applications to behavioral data.

\* \*\*08-simulation-and-reproducibility.ipynb\*\* — Monte Carlo simulation, statistical power analysis, design analysis, missing data, multiple imputation, reproducible workflows, reporting standards, publication tables and figures, and research-oriented Git workflows.

\* \*\*09-advanced-mixed-models.ipynb\*\* — Nonlinear mixed models, heterogeneous variance, spatial and temporal correlation, cross-classified models, multiple-membership models, multilevel hierarchies, dynamic mixed models, penalized mixed models, robust mixed models, and interpretation of increasingly complex hierarchical models.

\* \*\*10-bayesian-multilevel-models.ipynb\*\* — Bayesian multilevel modeling, hierarchical priors, posterior inference, posterior predictive checks, Bayesian model evaluation, comparison of frequentist and Bayesian approaches, cognitive science applications, and a complete mixed-effects analysis of a trial-level cognitive psychology dataset.
